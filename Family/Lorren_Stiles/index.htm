Thank you for your kind thoughts in this time of loss for the Stiles family. We truly appreciate your interest in making a donation on behalf of Lorren. If you are considering sending flowers we are instead asking you to make an equivalent contribution to Brigantine Wildlife Preserve .

 

Lorren loved birds and wildlife. Brigantine Wildlife Preserve is a place that he shared with many of us. He would pause to listen for a new bird song, or identify a critter he had not seen before. He taught us that it is the small things in life that are most important. Take some time today to notice something small and cherish the beauty that exists around you.

 

Brigantine Wilderness Area makes up about 6,600 acres of the more than 42,000 acres of Edwin B. Forsythe National Wildlife Refuge. It was established to preserve refuge wetlands for waterfowl, wading birds, shorebirds, and other wildlife. The wilderness area contains some of the last undeveloped barrier beaches along the Atlantic coast. And Little Beach Island is New Jersey's only undeveloped barrier island.
